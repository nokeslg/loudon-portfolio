Project Brief

In this project, we worked in groups of 2 to explore challenges faced by a specific user group within fintech. We explored financial struggles within a parent-focused user group — specifically how parents manage allowances, chores, and financial education for their children.

Discipline Visual Design / Branding
UI/UX Design
Context Interaction Design, University of Kansas
Year 2025
Problem

Parents struggle to find a simple, unified tool to manage their kids' allowances, track chores, and teach foundational money skills — all without relying on cash or juggling multiple apps.

How Might We

How might we help parents easily manage chores and allowances while teaching their kids about money?

App Preview
Solution

Provide parents with an easy way to manage chores, allowances, and their account balance — while teaching their kids about money.

Allowance Tracking
Monitor each child's balance, earning history, and weekly allowances in one place.
Bank Integration
Connect your bank account to manage funds and allocate allowances automatically.
Built-in Learning Platform
Age-appropriate lessons, articles, and podcasts to build financial literacy over time.
Chore Management
Assign, track, and reward chores with automatic balance updates when tasks are completed.
Kids Overview
A dedicated dashboard for parents to see each child's progress, tasks, and savings goals.
No Subscription
Core features available without a paywall — accessible to all families.

Key Insight

Parents don't just want to track money — they want a tool that helps them be intentional about raising financially aware kids. The emotional weight of that responsibility is the real design problem.

Ideation & Sketching

Before moving to digital, we sketched out initial flows and layouts to explore the information architecture and key interactions quickly.

Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes were used to rapidly test core flows — onboarding, chore assignment, and the allowance dashboard — before committing to the full visual design.

User Testing
The Task

We gave our users a task: add a new allowance for one of the children and add a new chore to the other.

Users ranked the experience 3, 3, 4 out of 10.

3/10
3/10

What Users Told Us
3/3 users got confused when trying to add a chore.
Overview section on the home screen was confusing.
2/3 users wanted to see a full money breakdown.
After User Testing
What we changed

Made it clear where to "Add a Chore" — moved from chore settings into the task list, where users naturally looked first.

Secondary fix

Primary and secondary button hierarchy was confusing during onboarding. Redesigned the button system for clearer visual priority throughout the app.
